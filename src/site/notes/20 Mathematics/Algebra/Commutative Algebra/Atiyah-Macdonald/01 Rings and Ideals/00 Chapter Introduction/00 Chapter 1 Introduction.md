# Chapter 1 Introduction

Chapter 1 supplies the language needed for the rest of the book. Its basic objects are [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/01 Rings and Ring Homomorphisms/Ring\|commutative rings with identity]], and its basic morphisms are [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/01 Rings and Ring Homomorphisms/Ring Homomorphism\|ring homomorphisms]] that preserve that identity.

The chapter has three movements:

1. build quotients from [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/02 Ideals and Quotient Rings/Ideal\|ideals]] and recognize quotients through [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/02 Ideals and Quotient Rings/Kernel of a Ring Homomorphism\|kernels]];
2. use [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/04 Prime and Maximal Ideals/Prime Ideal\|prime]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/04 Prime and Maximal Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|maximal ideals]] to detect domain-like and field-like quotients;
3. develop operations on ideals, including [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/06 Operations on Ideals/Sum of Ideals\|sum]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/06 Operations on Ideals/Product of Ideals\|product]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/06 Operations on Ideals/Ideal Quotient\|ideal quotient]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/06 Operations on Ideals/Radical of an Ideal\|radical]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/07 Extension and Contraction/Extension of an Ideal\|extension]], and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/07 Extension and Contraction/Contraction of an Ideal\|contraction]].

The exercises point toward algebraic geometry. They reinterpret prime ideals as points of the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/08 Exercises and Further Directions/Prime Spectrum\|Prime Spectrum]] and use the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/08 Exercises and Further Directions/Zariski Topology\|Zariski Topology]] to turn algebraic containment into geometric closure.

## Guiding idea

An element-by-element description of a ring is often too detailed. Ideals compress the information:

- quotienting by an ideal imposes equations;
- prime ideals detect multiplicative behavior;
- maximal ideals produce fields of residues;
- radicals forget multiplicity and retain vanishing information.

This is why the path from [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/02 Ideals and Quotient Rings/Quotient Ring\|Quotient Ring]] to [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/08 Exercises and Further Directions/Prime Spectrum\|Prime Spectrum]] is the conceptual backbone of the chapter.
