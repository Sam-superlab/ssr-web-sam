# Product of Ideals

For [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/02 Ideals and Quotient Rings/Ideal\|ideals]] $\mathfrak a,\mathfrak b$ in a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/01 Rings and Ring Homomorphisms/Ring\|Ring]] $A$, their **product** is

$$
\mathfrak a\mathfrak b
=
\left\{
\sum_{i=1}^r a_ib_i:
a_i\in\mathfrak a,\ b_i\in\mathfrak b
\right\}.
$$

It is the ideal generated by all simple products $ab$. A general element may require a sum of several such products.

## Properties

Ideal multiplication is commutative and associative, and

$$
\mathfrak a(\mathfrak b+\mathfrak c)
=
\mathfrak a\mathfrak b+\mathfrak a\mathfrak c.
$$

Also,

$$
\mathfrak a\mathfrak b\subseteq\mathfrak a\cap\mathfrak b.
$$

If the factors are [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/06 Operations on Ideals/Comaximal Ideal\|comaximal]], equality holds.

## Examples

- In $\mathbb Z$, $(m)(n)=(mn)$.
- $(x,y)(x,z)=(x^2,xy,xz,yz)$ in $k[x,y,z]$.
- If $\mathfrak m=(x_1,\ldots,x_n)\subseteq k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$, then $\mathfrak m^r$ consists of polynomials with no term of total degree below $r$.

Repeated products define the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/06 Operations on Ideals/Power of an Ideal\|Power of an Ideal]]. Products also appear in the finite-product criterion for a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/04 Prime and Maximal Ideals/Prime Ideal\|Prime Ideal]].
