# Algebraic Closure of a Field

An **algebraic closure** of a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/03 Zero Divisors Nilpotents and Units/Field\|Field]] $K$ is an algebraic field extension $\overline K/K$ in which every nonconstant polynomial over $\overline K$ splits into linear factors.

## Artin's construction

Exercise 13 sketches an existence proof.

For every monic irreducible polynomial $f\in K[t]$, introduce an indeterminate $x_f$. In the polynomial ring

$$
A=K[x_f:f\text{ monic irreducible}],
$$

let $\mathfrak a$ be generated by all $f(x_f)$. One proves $\mathfrak a$ is proper, places it inside a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/04 Prime and Maximal Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|Maximal Ideal]] $\mathfrak m$, and sets

$$
K_1=A/\mathfrak m.
$$

Then each irreducible polynomial over $K$ has a root in $K_1$.

Repeat the construction:

$$
K\subseteq K_1\subseteq K_2\subseteq\cdots.
$$

In the union $L=\bigcup_nK_n$, every polynomial appearing at some finite stage eventually splits. The subfield of elements of $L$ algebraic over $K$ is an algebraic closure of $K$.

## Conceptual role

The argument demonstrates how the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/04 Prime and Maximal Ideals/Maximal Ideal Theorem\|Maximal Ideal Theorem]] turns a consistent family of polynomial relations into a field in which those relations hold.

Algebraic closures exist and are unique up to a noncanonical $K$-isomorphism; the exercise addresses existence.
