# 02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules

A [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Submodule\|Submodule]] $N\subseteq M$ is an additive subgroup stable under the $A$-action. This stability makes the cosets $M/N$ into the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Quotient Module\|Quotient Module]] with $a(m+N)=am+N$.

Submodules of $M$ containing $N$ correspond, in an inclusion-preserving way, to submodules of $M/N$. The [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Correspondence Theorem for Modules\|Correspondence Theorem for Modules]] is the module analogue of the correspondence theorem for [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/02 Ideals and Quotient Rings/Ideal\|ideals]] in a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/02 Ideals and Quotient Rings/Quotient Ring\|Quotient Ring]].

For a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.01 Modules and Module Homomorphisms/Module Homomorphism\|Module Homomorphism]] $f:M\to P$:

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Kernel of a Module Homomorphism\|$\ker f$]] is the submodule sent to zero;
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Image of a Module Homomorphism\|$\operatorname{im}f$]] is the reached submodule of $P$;
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Cokernel\|$\operatorname{coker}f=P/\operatorname{im}f$]] records what remains after quotienting by the image.

If $N\subseteq\ker f$, then $f$ descends uniquely to a map $M/N\to P$. Taking $N=\ker f$ gives the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/First Isomorphism Theorem for Modules\|First Isomorphism Theorem for Modules]]:

$$M/\ker f\cong\operatorname{im}f.$$

## Intuition

Quotienting declares every element of $N$ to be zero. A map factors through $M/N$ precisely when it already treats all elements of $N$ as zero.
