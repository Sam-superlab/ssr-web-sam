# Cokernel

For an $A$-linear map $f:M\to N$, the **cokernel** is

$$\operatorname{coker}f=N/\operatorname{im}f.$$

It measures the failure of $f$ to be surjective: $\operatorname{coker}f=0$ exactly when $f$ is onto.

The [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Kernel of a Module Homomorphism\|kernel]] lives inside the domain and measures non-injectivity; the cokernel is a quotient of the codomain and measures non-surjectivity. In the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.06 Exact Sequences/Short Exact Sequence\|Short Exact Sequence]]

$$0\to M'\xrightarrow{f}M\xrightarrow{g}M''\to0,$$

the final module is canonically $M''\cong\operatorname{coker}f$.
