# Image of a Module Homomorphism

For an $A$-linear map $f:M\to N$, the **image** is

$$\operatorname{im}f=f(M)=\{f(m):m\in M\}.$$

It is a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Submodule\|Submodule]] of the codomain. The map is surjective exactly when $\operatorname{im}f=N$.

The image is naturally isomorphic to the domain after removing the relations invisible to the map:

$$\operatorname{im}f\cong M/\ker f.$$

The quotient $N/\operatorname{im}f$ is the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Cokernel\|Cokernel]]. In an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.06 Exact Sequences/Exact Sequence\|Exact Sequence]], image and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/02 Modules/02.02 Submodules and Quotient Modules/Kernel of a Module Homomorphism\|kernel]] are compared at each intermediate term.
