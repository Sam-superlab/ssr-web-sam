# Localization of Primary Ideals

Let $\mathfrak q$ be a $\mathfrak p$-[[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/04 Primary Decomposition/01 Primary Decomposition/Primary Ideal\|Primary Ideal]] and $S$ a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/03 Rings and Modules of Fractions/01 Localization/Multiplicative Set\|Multiplicative Set]].

- If $S\cap\mathfrak p\ne\varnothing$, then $S^{-1}\mathfrak q=S^{-1}A$.
- If $S\cap\mathfrak p=\varnothing$, then $S^{-1}\mathfrak q$ is $S^{-1}\mathfrak p$-primary and contracts to $\mathfrak q$.

The first case holds because a power of an element in $S\cap\mathfrak p$ lies in $\mathfrak q$ and becomes a unit. In the second, $as\in\mathfrak q$ with $s\notin\mathfrak p$ forces $a\in\mathfrak q$.

## Effect on a decomposition

Localizing a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/04 Primary Decomposition/01 Primary Decomposition/Primary Decomposition\|Primary Decomposition]] deletes the components whose radicals meet $S$ and preserves the others. Localizing at an isolated associated prime and contracting recovers its unique primary component.
