# Symbolic Power

For a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/01 Rings and Ideals/04 Prime and Maximal Ideals/Prime Ideal\|Prime Ideal]] $\mathfrak p$ of $A$, its $n$th **symbolic power** is

$$
\mathfrak p^{(n)}
=\mathfrak p^nA_{\mathfrak p}\cap A.
$$

It is the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/03 Rings and Modules of Fractions/03 Extended and Contracted Ideals/Saturation of an Ideal\|saturation]] of $\mathfrak p^n$ with respect to $A\setminus\mathfrak p$ and is $\mathfrak p$-primary.

## Relation to ordinary powers

Always $\mathfrak p^n\subseteq\mathfrak p^{(n)}$. Equality holds exactly when $\mathfrak p^n$ is already $\mathfrak p$-primary. If $\mathfrak p^n$ has a primary decomposition, $\mathfrak p^{(n)}$ is its $\mathfrak p$-primary component.

Symbolic powers isolate behavior along $\mathfrak p$ while discarding components supported at larger primes.
