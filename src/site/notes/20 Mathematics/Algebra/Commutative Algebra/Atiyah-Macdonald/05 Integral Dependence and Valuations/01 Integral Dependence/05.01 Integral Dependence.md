# 05.01 Integral Dependence

## Monic equations

Let $A\subseteq B$. An element $x\in B$ is an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/01 Integral Dependence/Integral Element\|Integral Element]] over $A$ if it satisfies

$$
x^n+a_1x^{n-1}+\cdots+a_n=0,
\qquad a_i\in A.
$$

The leading coefficient must be $1$. Algebraicity alone is weaker: for example, $1/2\in\mathbb Q$ is algebraic but not integral over $\mathbb Z$.

## Finite-module criterion

The following are equivalent:

1. $x$ is integral over $A$.
2. $A[x]$ is finitely generated as an $A$-module.
3. $A[x]$ is contained in a subring of $B$ finite as an $A$-module.
4. There is a faithful $A[x]$-module finite as an $A$-module.

The forward direction reduces all powers of $x$ to the span of $1,x,\ldots,x^{n-1}$. The reverse direction uses the determinant trick on multiplication by $x$.

## Integral closure and transitivity

Finitely many integral elements generate a finite $A$-module. Hence sums, differences, and products of integral elements remain integral, so they form the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/01 Integral Dependence/Integral Closure\|Integral Closure]] of $A$ in $B$.

[[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/01 Integral Dependence/Integral Dependence\|Integral Dependence]] is transitive: if $B$ is integral over $A$ and $C$ is integral over $B$, then $C$ is integral over $A$. The proof collects the finitely many coefficients of a monic equation for $x\in C$ and builds a finite $A$-module containing $x$.

## Stability under basic operations

If $B$ is integral over $A$, then:

- $B/\mathfrak b$ is integral over $A/(A\cap\mathfrak b)$;
- $S^{-1}B$ is integral over $S^{-1}A$ for every [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/03 Rings and Modules of Fractions/01 Localization/Multiplicative Set\|Multiplicative Set]] $S$.

These facts let prime questions be reduced by quotienting and localizing.
