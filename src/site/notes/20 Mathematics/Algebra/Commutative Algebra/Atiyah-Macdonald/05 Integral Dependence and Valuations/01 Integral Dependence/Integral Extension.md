# Integral Extension

A ring extension $A\subseteq B$ is **integral** if every $b\in B$ is an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/01 Integral Dependence/Integral Element\|Integral Element]] over $A$. More generally, a homomorphism $A\to B$ is integral when $B$ is integral over the image of $A$.

## Finite versus integral

If $B$ is finite as an $A$-module, then $B$ is integral over $A$. Conversely, an integral algebra of finite type over $A$ is module-finite. An integral extension need not be module-finite when infinitely many integral generators are required.

## Stability

- A composite of integral extensions is integral.
- Quotients of integral extensions are integral.
- Localizations of integral extensions are integral.
- Base change preserves integrality.

## Prime consequences

Integral extensions satisfy [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/02 Going Up/Lying-Over Theorem\|lying over]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/02 Going Up/Incomparability Theorem\|incomparability]], and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/02 Going Up/Going-Up Theorem\|going up]]. Maximal ideals correspond under contraction: if $\mathfrak q\subseteq B$ is prime and $\mathfrak p=\mathfrak q\cap A$, then $\mathfrak q$ is maximal exactly when $\mathfrak p$ is maximal.
