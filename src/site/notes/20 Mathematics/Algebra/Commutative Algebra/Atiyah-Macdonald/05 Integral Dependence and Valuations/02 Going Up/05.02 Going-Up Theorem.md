# 05.02 Going-Up Theorem

Let $A\subseteq B$ be an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/01 Integral Dependence/Integral Extension\|Integral Extension]]. Integrality imposes three related constraints on prime ideals.

## Fields and maximal ideals

If $A\subseteq B$ are domains and $B$ is integral over $A$, then $A$ is a field exactly when $B$ is. Applying this to $A/\mathfrak p\subseteq B/\mathfrak q$ shows that a prime $\mathfrak q$ of $B$ is maximal exactly when its contraction $\mathfrak p$ is maximal.

## Incomparability

If $\mathfrak q\subseteq\mathfrak q'$ are primes of $B$ with the same contraction in $A$, then $\mathfrak q=\mathfrak q'$. Thus distinct primes over one base prime cannot contain one another; see [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/02 Going Up/Incomparability Theorem\|Incomparability Theorem]].

## Lying over

For every prime $\mathfrak p\subseteq A$, some prime $\mathfrak q\subseteq B$ contracts to $\mathfrak p$. The proof localizes at $\mathfrak p$, chooses a maximal ideal of $B_{\mathfrak p}$, and uses the maximal-ideal result. This is the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/02 Going Up/Lying-Over Theorem\|Lying-Over Theorem]].

## Going up

Given an ascending chain

$$
\mathfrak p_1\subseteq\cdots\subseteq\mathfrak p_n
$$

and a partial chain of primes of $B$ lying above its first terms, the chain upstairs can be extended to the same length. Reduce to two primes, quotient by the lower pair, apply lying over, and lift back.
