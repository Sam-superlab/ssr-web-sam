# Incomparability Theorem

Let $A\subseteq B$ be an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/05 Integral Dependence and Valuations/01 Integral Dependence/Integral Extension\|Integral Extension]]. If prime ideals $\mathfrak q\subseteq\mathfrak q'$ of $B$ have the same contraction to $A$, then

$$
\mathfrak q=\mathfrak q'.
$$

Thus two distinct primes lying over one prime downstairs are incomparable under inclusion.

## Proof idea

Localize at the common contraction $\mathfrak p$. Both extended primes contract to the unique maximal ideal of $A_{\mathfrak p}$. In an integral extension, primes above a maximal ideal are maximal. The inclusion between two maximal ideals must be equality, and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Atiyah-Macdonald/03 Rings and Modules of Fractions/03 Extended and Contracted Ideals/Prime Correspondence Under Localization\|Prime Correspondence Under Localization]] brings the equality back to $B$.
