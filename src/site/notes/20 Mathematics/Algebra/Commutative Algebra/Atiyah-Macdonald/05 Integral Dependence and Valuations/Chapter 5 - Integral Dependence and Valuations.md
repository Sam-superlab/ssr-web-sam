# Chapter 5 - Integral Dependence and Valuations

Integral dependence describes elements that satisfy monic equations over a smaller ring. It is the algebra behind normalization and strongly constrains how prime ideals behave in an extension. Valuation rings then provide a powerful test for integral closure.

## Logical flow

A monic equation makes $A[x]$ finite as an $A$-module. That finiteness allows determinant arguments, proves transitivity, and survives quotients and localization. Once $B$ is integral over $A$, primes lie over primes and ascending chains lift. Descending chains lift under the additional assumptions that the rings are domains and $A$ is integrally closed. Finally, the integral closure of $A$ in a field is the intersection of all valuation rings containing $A$.
