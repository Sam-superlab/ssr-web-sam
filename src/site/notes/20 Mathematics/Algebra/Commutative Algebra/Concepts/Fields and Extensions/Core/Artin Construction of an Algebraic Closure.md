# Artin Construction of an Algebraic Closure

Artin's construction proves that every [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Elements and Special Rings/Field\|Field]] $K$ admits an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Fields and Extensions/Core/Algebraic Closure\|Algebraic Closure]]. The idea is to adjoin roots of all irreducible polynomials simultaneously and then repeat the construction.

## Artin's construction

Exercise 13 sketches an existence proof.

For every monic irreducible polynomial $f\in K[t]$, introduce an indeterminate $x_f$. In the polynomial ring

$$
A=K[x_f:f\text{ monic irreducible}],
$$

let $\mathfrak a$ be generated by all $f(x_f)$. One proves $\mathfrak a$ is proper, places it inside a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|Maximal Ideal]] $\mathfrak m$, and sets

$$
K_1=A/\mathfrak m.
$$

Then each irreducible polynomial over $K$ has a root in $K_1$.

Repeat the construction:

$$
K\subseteq K_1\subseteq K_2\subseteq\cdots.
$$

In the union $L=\bigcup_nK_n$, every polynomial appearing at some finite stage eventually splits. The subfield of elements of $L$ algebraic over $K$ is an algebraic closure of $K$.

## Conceptual role

The argument demonstrates how the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Krull's Maximal Ideal Theorem\|Krull's Maximal Ideal Theorem]] turns a consistent family of polynomial relations into a field in which those relations hold.

The construction establishes existence. Uniqueness up to a generally noncanonical $K$-isomorphism belongs to the general theory summarized on [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Fields and Extensions/Core/Algebraic Closure\|Algebraic Closure]].
