# Tensor Product

For $A$-modules $M,N$, the **tensor product** $M\otimes_A N$ is an $A$-module generated by symbols $x\otimes y$, subject to bilinearity relations. Its defining feature is the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Tensor Products/Tensor Product Universal Property\|Tensor Product Universal Property]].

## Intuition

Tensoring creates a universal home for products that are linear separately in two inputs. The balancing relation

$$(ax)\otimes y=x\otimes(ay)$$

lets a scalar move from one factor to the other.

## Basic computations

- $A\otimes_A M\cong M$ by $a\otimes m\mapsto am$.
- $(A/I)\otimes_A M\cong M/IM$.
- $\mathbb Z/m\mathbb Z\otimes_{\mathbb Z}\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z\cong\mathbb Z/\gcd(m,n)\mathbb Z$.

## Pitfalls

Not every element is a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Tensor Products/Pure Tensor\|Pure Tensor]]; general elements are finite sums $\sum x_i\otimes y_i$. Also, inclusions $M'\subseteq M$ and $N'\subseteq N$ need not induce an inclusion $M'\otimes N'\subseteq M\otimes N$. That failure motivates [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Flatness and Tor/Flat Module\|flatness]].

The same module tensor product carries a natural ring structure when both factors are algebras; see [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Algebras over Rings/Core/Tensor Product of Algebras\|Tensor Product of Algebras]].
