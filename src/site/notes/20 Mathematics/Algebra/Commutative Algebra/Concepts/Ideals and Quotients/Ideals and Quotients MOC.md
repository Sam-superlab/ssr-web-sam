# Ideals and Quotients

Ideals encode equations inside a ring. They support an internal arithmetic, define quotient rings, and identify the prime and maximal points from which spectra and local algebra are built.

## Generating and combining ideals

- Begin with [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Ideal\|Ideal]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Zero Ideal\|Zero Ideal]], and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Principal Ideal\|Principal Ideal]].
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Sum of Ideals\|Sum of Ideals]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Intersection of Ideals\|Intersection of Ideals]] give the join and meet operations.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Product of Ideals\|Product of Ideals]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Power of an Ideal\|Power of an Ideal]] carry multiplicative information.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Ideal Quotient\|Ideal Quotient]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Annihilator in a Ring\|Annihilator in a Ring]] measure divisibility and vanishing.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Comaximal Ideal\|Comaximal Ideal]] leads to the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Chinese Remainder Theorem\|Chinese Remainder Theorem]].
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Finite Prime Avoidance\|Finite Prime Avoidance]] provides simultaneous avoidance of finitely many prime ideals.

## Quotients and correspondence

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Congruence Modulo an Ideal\|Congruence Modulo an Ideal]] is equality inside a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Quotient Ring\|Quotient Ring]].
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Canonical Projection\|Canonical Projection]] is the universal map from a ring to its quotient.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Correspondence Theorem for Ideals\|Correspondence Theorem for Ideals]] identifies ideals of a quotient with ideals above the kernel.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Extension of an Ideal\|Extension of an Ideal]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Contraction of an Ideal\|Contraction of an Ideal]] transport ideals across a ring map.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Quotients and Correspondence/Extension-Contraction Correspondence\|Extension-Contraction Correspondence]] records their order-theoretic relationship.

## Prime, maximal, and radical ideals

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Prime Ideal\|Prime Ideal]] is detected by a domain quotient.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|Maximal Ideal]] is detected by a field quotient.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Krull's Maximal Ideal Theorem\|Krull's Maximal Ideal Theorem]] guarantees maximal ideals above proper ideals.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Minimal Prime Ideal\|Minimal Prime Ideal]] identifies the smallest prime components.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Radical of an Ideal\|Radical of an Ideal]] forgets multiplicity; [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Radical as an Intersection of Prime Ideals\|Radical as an Intersection of Prime Ideals]] gives its prime description.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Nilradical\|Nilradical]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Jacobson Radical\|Jacobson Radical]] are canonical intersections of prime and maximal ideals.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Jacobson Radical Unit Test\|Jacobson Radical Unit Test]] recognizes the Jacobson radical through invertibility.
