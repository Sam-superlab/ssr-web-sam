# Power of an Ideal

For an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Ideal\|Ideal]] $\mathfrak a$ in a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Foundations and Morphisms/Ring\|Ring]] $A$, define

$$
\mathfrak a^0=A=(1),
$$

and for $n>0$,

$$
\mathfrak a^n
=
\underbrace{\mathfrak a\cdots\mathfrak a}_{n\text{ factors}}.
$$

It is generated by all products $x_1\cdots x_n$ with each $x_i\in\mathfrak a$.

## Basic behavior

$$
\mathfrak a^{m+n}=\mathfrak a^m\mathfrak a^n,
\qquad
\mathfrak a^{n+1}\subseteq\mathfrak a^n.
$$

The descending chain need not stabilize.

## Examples

- In $\mathbb Z$, $(m)^n=(m^n)$.
- If $\mathfrak m=(x_1,\ldots,x_r)$ in a polynomial ring, then $\mathfrak m^n$ is generated by monomials of total degree $n$ and contains exactly the polynomials with no terms of degree below $n$.
- In $k[\varepsilon]/(\varepsilon^2)$, $(\varepsilon)^2=(0)$.

## Radicals ignore powers

For every ideal,

$$
\sqrt{\mathfrak a^n}=\sqrt{\mathfrak a}.
$$

In particular, if $\mathfrak p$ is [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Prime Ideal\|prime]], then $\sqrt{\mathfrak p^n}=\mathfrak p$.
