# Integral Dependence

**Integral dependence** is the relation in which an element of a larger ring satisfies a monic equation over a smaller ring. A ring extension is integral when every element upstairs is an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Integral Extensions and Normalization/Integral Elements and Extensions/Integral Element\|Integral Element]] over the base.

## Closure properties

- Sums, differences, and products of integral elements are integral.
- Integral dependence is transitive.
- It is preserved by quotients.
- It is preserved by [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Localization and Fractions/Core/Localization of a Ring\|localization]].
- An algebra that is both finitely generated as an algebra and integral is finite as a module.

The finite-module viewpoint is the unifying reason: finitely many integral generators produce a module-finite algebra.

## Geometric role

Integral maps behave like finite-valued coverings: primes exist over every base prime by [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Integral Extensions and Normalization/Prime Spectra under Integral Extensions/Lying-Over Theorem\|lying over]], ascending chains lift by [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Integral Extensions and Normalization/Prime Spectra under Integral Extensions/Going-Up Theorem\|going up]], and under normality descending chains lift by [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Integral Extensions and Normalization/Prime Spectra under Integral Extensions/Going-Down Theorem\|going down]].
