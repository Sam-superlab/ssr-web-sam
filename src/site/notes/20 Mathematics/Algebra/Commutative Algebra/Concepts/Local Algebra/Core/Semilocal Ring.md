# Semilocal Ring

A **semilocal ring** is a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Foundations and Morphisms/Ring\|Ring]] with only finitely many [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|maximal ideals]]. Every [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Local Algebra/Core/Local Ring\|Local Ring]] is semilocal, but not conversely.

## Examples

- A field has one maximal ideal, so it is local and semilocal.
- $\mathbb Z/12\mathbb Z$ has maximal ideals generated by the classes of $2$ and $3$, so it is semilocal but not local.
- More generally, $\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z$ has one maximal ideal for each distinct prime divisor of $n$.
- $\mathbb Z$ is not semilocal because it has infinitely many maximal ideals $(p)$.

## Why finite maximal spectra help

If the maximal ideals $\mathfrak m_1,\ldots,\mathfrak m_r$ are pairwise distinct, then they are pairwise [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Comaximal Ideal\|comaximal]]. The [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Chinese Remainder Theorem\|Chinese Remainder Theorem]] can then combine congruence information modulo their powers under suitable hypotheses.

The term is also written “semi-local.” This knowledge base uses the single-word spelling in filenames for consistency.
