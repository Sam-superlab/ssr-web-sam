# Local Algebra

Local algebra studies a ring near one maximal or prime ideal. In this setting, units are easy to recognize, finite generation interacts strongly with the residue field, and global questions can often be reduced to local ones.

## Local and semilocal rings

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Local Algebra/Core/Local Ring\|Local Ring]] has one maximal ideal; [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Local Algebra/Core/Semilocal Ring\|Semilocal Ring]] has finitely many.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Local Algebra/Core/Residue Field\|Residue Field]] is the quotient by a maximal ideal.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Elements and Special Rings/Unit\|Unit]] is characterized in a local ring by avoidance of the maximal ideal.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Jacobson Radical\|Jacobson Radical]] equals the maximal ideal in the local case.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Jacobson Radical Unit Test\|Jacobson Radical Unit Test]] turns radical membership into an invertibility criterion.

## Passing to a local ring

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Localization and Fractions/Core/Localization of a Ring\|Localization of a Ring]] at the complement of a prime produces its local ring.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Localization and Fractions/Core/Multiplicative Set\|Multiplicative Set]] controls which elements become invertible.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Localization and Support/Prime Correspondence Under Localization\|Prime Correspondence Under Localization]] describes the remaining prime ideals.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Localization and Fractions/Ideals/Saturation by a Multiplicative Set\|Saturation by a Multiplicative Set]] describes what extension followed by contraction remembers.

## Local-to-global methods

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Local Algebra/Local-Global Methods/Local-to-Global Property\|Local-to-Global Property]] distinguishes statements detectable at all prime or maximal localizations.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Localization and Support/Support of a Module\|Support of a Module]] records the primes where a module is visible.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Localization and Fractions/Modules/Exactness of Localization\|Exactness of Localization]] lets kernels, cokernels, and exact sequences be tested locally.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Flatness and Tor/Flat Module\|Flat Module]] is a central local property, with [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Flatness and Tor/Ideal Criterion for Flatness\|Ideal Criterion for Flatness]] as an effective test.

## Finite generation over local rings

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Finitely Generated Module\|Finitely Generated Module]] is the setting for the strongest local generation results.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Nakayama Lemma\|Nakayama Lemma]] removes terms lying in the maximal ideal.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Minimal Number of Generators over a Local Ring\|Minimal Number of Generators over a Local Ring]] is measured over the residue field.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Determinant Trick\|Determinant Trick]] supplies a reusable proof mechanism behind Nakayama-type results.
