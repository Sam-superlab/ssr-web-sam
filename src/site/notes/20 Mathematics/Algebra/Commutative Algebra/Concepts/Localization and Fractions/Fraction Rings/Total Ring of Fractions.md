# Total Ring of Fractions

The **total ring of fractions** of a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Foundations and Morphisms/Ring\|Ring]] $A$ is $S^{-1}A$, where $S$ is the set of all non-zero-divisors of $A$.

For an integral domain this is the ordinary [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Localization and Fractions/Fraction Rings/Field of Fractions\|Field of Fractions]]. In a ring with zero-divisors it need not be a field, but the map $A\to S^{-1}A$ is injective and every element of the localization is either a unit or a zero-divisor.

## Example

For $A=k\times k$, every non-zero-divisor is already a unit, so the total ring of fractions is $A$ itself. For a reduced ring with several components, the total fraction ring often behaves like a product of the fraction fields of those components, subject to suitable finiteness hypotheses.
