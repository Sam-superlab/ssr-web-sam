# Product of an Ideal and a Module

For an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Ideal\|Ideal]] $\mathfrak a\subseteq A$ and an $A$-module $M$, define

$$\mathfrak aM=\left\{\sum_{j=1}^r a_jm_j:a_j\in\mathfrak a,\ m_j\in M\right\}.$$

This is a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Submodules and Quotients/Submodule\|Submodule]] of $M$. It is generated by all scalar products $am$ with $a\in\mathfrak a$ and $m\in M$.

## Examples

- For $M=A$, this construction recovers the ideal $\mathfrak a$.
- If $M=A/I$, then $\mathfrak aM=(\mathfrak a+I)/I$.
- The quotient $M/\mathfrak aM$ is naturally a module over $A/\mathfrak a$.

This operation is central in [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Nakayama Lemma\|Nakayama Lemma]] and in the tensor identity $(A/\mathfrak a)\otimes_A M\cong M/\mathfrak aM$.

Unlike ideals, two arbitrary submodules of one module cannot generally be multiplied: a module has scalar multiplication, not multiplication between its elements.
