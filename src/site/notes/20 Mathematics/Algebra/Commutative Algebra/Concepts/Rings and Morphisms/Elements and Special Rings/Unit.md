# Unit

An element $x$ of a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Foundations and Morphisms/Ring\|Ring]] $A$ is a **unit** if there exists $y\in A$ such that

$$
xy=1.
$$

The inverse is unique and is written $x^{-1}$. The units form an abelian group under multiplication, commonly denoted $A^\times$.

## Equivalent ideal test

The [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Principal Ideal\|Principal Ideal]] generated by $x$ satisfies

$$
x\text{ is a unit}
\iff
(x)=A
\iff
1\in(x).
$$

Indeed, $1=ax$ is precisely an inverse equation.

## Examples

- The units of $\mathbb Z$ are $\pm1$.
- The units of a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Elements and Special Rings/Field\|Field]] are exactly its nonzero elements.
- In $\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z$, the class of $a$ is a unit exactly when $\gcd(a,n)=1$.
- In a polynomial ring $k[x]$ over a field, the units are the nonzero constants.

## Useful criteria

- Every element outside the unique maximal ideal of a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Local Algebra/Core/Local Ring\|Local Ring]] is a unit.
- An element is a nonunit exactly when it lies in some maximal ideal; this follows from the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Krull's Maximal Ideal Theorem\|Krull's Maximal Ideal Theorem]].
- If $x$ is nilpotent, then $1-x$ is a unit with inverse $1+x+\cdots+x^{n-1}$ once $x^n=0$.
- The [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Jacobson Radical Unit Test\|Jacobson Radical Unit Test]] says $x\in J(A)$ exactly when $1-xy$ is a unit for every $y\in A$.
