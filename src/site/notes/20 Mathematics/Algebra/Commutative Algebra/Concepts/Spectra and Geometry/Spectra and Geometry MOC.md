# Spectra and Geometry

The prime spectrum turns inclusion and vanishing among ideals into topology. Ring maps reverse direction on spectra, localization selects open or local pieces, and coordinate rings translate affine geometry into algebra.

## The spectrum and its topology

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Core/Prime Spectrum\|Prime Spectrum]] uses prime ideals as points.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Core/Zariski Topology\|Zariski Topology]] translates ideals into closed sets.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Core/Basic Open Set\|Basic Open Set]] supplies the standard basis of opens.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Core/Maximal Spectrum\|Maximal Spectrum]] retains only the closed points associated with maximal ideals.

- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Prime Ideal\|Prime Ideal]], [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|Maximal Ideal]], and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Minimal Prime Ideal\|Minimal Prime Ideal]] play distinct geometric roles.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Irreducibility/Irreducible Topological Space\|Irreducible Topological Space]] captures spaces that cannot be split into two proper closed pieces.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Spectra and Geometry/Irreducibility/Irreducible Component\|Irreducible Component]] gives the maximal irreducible pieces of a spectrum.
- [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Nilradical\|Nilradical]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Radical as an Intersection of Prime Ideals\|Radical as an Intersection of Prime Ideals]] explain why spectra ignore nilpotent thickening.
