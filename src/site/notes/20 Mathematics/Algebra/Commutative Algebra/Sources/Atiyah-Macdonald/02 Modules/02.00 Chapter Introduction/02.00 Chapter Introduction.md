# 02.00 Chapter Introduction

Chapter 2 shifts attention from [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Ideal\|ideals]] to [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Foundations/Module\|modules]]. This creates useful “elbow room”: an ideal $I\subseteq A$ and the quotient $A/I$ look different as rings, but both are naturally $A$-modules and can be studied by the same linear methods.

The chapter has three stages:

1. develop modules, their maps, subobjects, quotients, and finiteness;
2. encode relations using [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Exact Sequences/Exact Sequence\|exact sequences]];
3. construct the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Tensor Products/Tensor Product\|Tensor Product]] and study when tensoring preserves exact information.

This viewpoint is central because many later questions in commutative algebra are really questions about modules: generators of an ideal, relations among generators, passage along a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Rings and Morphisms/Foundations and Morphisms/Ring Homomorphism\|Ring Homomorphism]], and local tests after changing scalars.

## Guiding example

For an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Operations/Ideal\|Ideal]] $I\subseteq A$, the short sequence

$$0\longrightarrow I\longrightarrow A\longrightarrow A/I\longrightarrow 0$$

records inclusion, quotient, and the relation between them in one object. Tensoring this sequence later explains formulas such as $(A/I)\otimes_A M\cong M/IM$.
