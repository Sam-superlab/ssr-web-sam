# 02.12 Exercises

The Chapter 2 exercises extend the main text in four directions. This note is a roadmap, not a reproduction of the problem set.

## 1. Tensor calculations and flatness (Exercises 1-8)

The opening problems develop working formulas such as

$$(A/\mathfrak a)\otimes_A M\cong M/\mathfrak aM$$

and test tensor products over local rings. They show that direct sums of flat modules are flat, polynomial rings are flat over their coefficient rings, and flatness composes under suitable changes of scalars.

## 2. Finite generation and free rank (Exercises 9-13)

These exercises use [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Nakayama Lemma\|Nakayama Lemma]] and [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Exact Sequences/Short Exact Sequence\|short exact sequences]] to pass finite generation between terms. Reduction modulo a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Ideals and Quotients/Prime Maximal and Radical Ideals/Maximal Ideal\|Maximal Ideal]] proves invariant basis number: if $A\neq0$ and $A^m\cong A^n$, then $m=n$. Splittings of surjections onto a finite [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Finite Generation and Local Methods/Free Module\|Free Module]] are also explored.

## 3. Direct limits (Exercises 14-23)

A [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Direct Constructions and Limits/Directed Set\|Directed Set]] indexes a [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Direct Constructions and Limits/Direct System\|Direct System]]. Its [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Direct Constructions and Limits/Direct Limit\|Direct Limit]] is built from a direct sum modulo the relations identifying each element with its later images. The exercises prove the universal property, [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Modules/Direct Constructions and Limits/Exactness of Direct Limits\|Exactness of Direct Limits]], and that the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Tensor Products/Tensor Product\|Tensor Product]] [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Tensor Products/Tensor Product Commutes with Direct Limits\|commutes with direct limits]]. Direct limits of rings and tensor products of arbitrary families of algebras are constructed similarly.

## 4. Tor and absolute flatness (Exercises 24-28)

The [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Flatness and Tor/Tor Functor\|Tor Functor]] detects failure of tensor exactness. Vanishing of $\operatorname{Tor}_1$ characterizes flatness and reduces to the [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Flatness and Tor/Ideal Criterion for Flatness\|Ideal Criterion for Flatness]]. The final problems characterize an [[20 Mathematics/Algebra/Commutative Algebra/Concepts/Homological Algebra/Flatness and Tor/Absolutely Flat Ring\|Absolutely Flat Ring]] through idempotent principal ideals and direct-summand finitely generated ideals.

## Study strategy

First solve a representative tensor calculation, then one exact-sequence problem, then Exercises 14-20 as a continuous direct-limit unit. Treat Exercises 24-28 as an optional homological-algebra extension if Tor has not yet been introduced elsewhere.
