# 1) What a Shakespearean sonnet looks like (form)

- **14 lines** in **iambic pentameter** (see §4).
    
- **Rhyme scheme:** **ABAB CDCD EFEF GG** (the last two lines are a **couplet**).
    
- **Volta (“turn”)** = a shift in idea or emotion. Often at **line 9** (after the second quatrain) or at **line 13** (the couplet).
    
- **Typical flow:**  
    Quatrain 1 = topic → Quatrain 2 = develop/complicate → Quatrain 3 = twist/pressure → Couplet = punchline, solution, or sting.
    

# 2) How the language “moves” (what trips readers up)

- **Flexible word order (inversion):** Shakespeare often puts verbs/objects **before** subjects for rhythm/rhyme.  
    _Ex._ “**Gives** he his heart” → “He gives his heart.”
    
- **Omitted words (ellipsis):** Little words are dropped if meaning is clear.  
    _Ex._ “I (do) love thee” → “I love thee.”
    
- **Compressed spelling (elision/contraction):** to fit meter: **o’er**(=over), **e’en**(=even), **ne’er**(=never), **’twas**(=it was).
    
- **Multiple meanings & puns:** A single word can mean 2–3 things at once (love/“prove,” “will”= desire + future, etc.).
    
- **Rhetorical patterns:** **Antithesis** (love vs. time), **lists**, **repetition** (anaphora), **metaphor** and extended **conceits**.
    

# 3) Quick grammar map (thou/you + verb endings)

**Second-person pronouns**

- **thou** (subject, singular, informal/intimate) → “**Thou** art kind.”
    
- **thee** (object) → “I love **thee**.”
    
- **thy/thine** (your/yours) → “**Thy** eyes,” “**Thine** honor.”
    
- **you/your** = plural or polite singular (respect/distance).
    

**Common verb forms**

- **art** = are; **wert** = were.
    
- **hast**/**dost**/**wilt**/**shalt**/**shouldst**/**wouldst**/**couldst** = have/do/will/shall/should/would/could (for **thou**).
    
- **-th endings** with **he/she/it**: **hath**(has), **doth**(does), “goeth” (goes).
    

**Handy little words**

- **ere** = before; **oft** = often; **anon** = soon; **betimes** = early; **whence** = from where; **whither** = to where; **hence** = from here/therefore (context decides).
    

# 4) Sound & rhythm (iambic pentameter made simple)

- **Iamb** = 2 beats: **da-DUM** (unstressed → stressed).
    
- **Pentameter** = 5 iambs per line (≈ **10 syllables**): da-DUM ×5.
    
- Sometimes he **breaks** the pattern on purpose (extra syllable, a trochee **DUM-da** at the start, a pause **caesura**).
    
- **-ed** can be two syllables for rhythm: **bless-èd**, **belov-èd**.
    

# 5) Mini-glossary for fast decoding

- **approve/prove** = test, demonstrate the truth of.
    
- **presently** = immediately/soon (context).
    
- **secure** = make safe/assure (not always “lock”).
    
- **conceit** = extended metaphor or imaginative idea.
    
- **issue** = outcome/offspring/result.
    
- **physic** = medicine/cure.
    
- **temper** = mix, moderate, or character.
    
- **wrack/wreck** = ruin, destruction.
    
- **humor** = mood/temperament.
    
- **slander** = false, damaging speech (reputation theme).
    
- **vulgar** = common, ordinary (not “rude” only).
    
- **sweet**/**fair** often = beautiful/pleasing (not just taste/just/justice).
    
- **mark** (verb) = notice, pay attention.
    

# 6) “Translate” any sonnet—step-by-step

1. **Find the sentence core:** underline **subject–verb–object**.
    
2. **Undo inversions:** rewrite into modern order (“Gives he” → “He gives”).
    
3. **Expand contractions & fill ellipses:** write out missing words.
    
4. **Swap old words:** use the glossary (e.g., “ere”→“before”).
    
5. **Track the argument by quatrain:** What changes at lines **5, 9, 13**? Where’s the **volta**?
    
6. **Paraphrase each quatrain in 1 plain sentence**, then the couplet as the “takeaway.”
    
7. **Check tone** (admiring, jealous, defiant?) and **who speaks to whom** (beloved, rival, Time, the Muse).
    

# 7) Sample micro-rewrites (showing the method)

- **Original-style:** “**Gives** he his truth to **Time**, that **eats** all things.”  
    **Modern order:** “He gives his truth to Time, which eats all things.”  
    **Plain paraphrase:** People trust Time, but Time destroys.
    
- **Original-style:** “**Ne’er** did **thy** beauty **prove** my constancy.”  
    **Modern order:** “Your beauty never tested my loyalty.”  
    **Plain paraphrase:** My loyalty didn’t depend on your looks.
    

# 8) Common traps (and fixes)

- **Trap:** thinking each line is a full sentence → **Fix:** sentences can run across lines (**enjambment**).
    
- **Trap:** translating word-for-word → **Fix:** find the **idea** first, then rephrase.
    
- **Trap:** missing irony or antithesis → **Fix:** list the **opposites** (time vs. youth, truth vs. appearance).
    
- **Trap:** assuming modern meanings → **Fix:** check the **EME** sense (see glossary above).

# 1) 形式（Form）

- **14 lines**，**iambic pentameter（抑扬五音步）**。
    
- **Rhyme scheme**: **ABAB CDCD EFEF GG**（最后两行是**couplet（对句）**）。
    
- **Volta（转折点）**：思想/情感转弯，常见在**第9行**或**最后对句**。
    
- 常见逻辑：Q1 立题 → Q2 推进/复杂化 → Q3 反转/施压 → Couplet 总结/反讽/点题。
    

# 2) 常见语言“坑”（Reading Tricks）

- **Inversion（倒装）**：为押韵/节奏把顺序打乱。  
    例：**Gives he his heart** → **He gives his heart**。
    
- **Ellipsis（省略）**：小词被省。I **(do)** love thee → I love thee。
    
- **Elision（为合拍的省写）**：o’er=over, ne’er=never, ’twas=it was。
    
- **Puns & double meanings（双关/一词多义）**：如 _prove_ = 证明/考验；_will_ = 意志/欲望/将来。
    
- **Rhetoric（修辞）**：antithesis（对立：love vs. time）、lists、anaphora（首语重复）、extended **metaphor/conceit（扩展隐喻/奇喻）**。
    

# 3) 二人称速查（Thou/You）

- **thou**（主格，亲密/单数）：“**Thou** art kind.”  
    **thee**（宾格）：“I love **thee**.”  
    **thy/thine**（你的/你的东西）：“**Thy** eyes,” “**Thine** honor.”
    
- **you/your**：复数或礼貌单数（更正式/疏离）。
    
- 动词结尾：he/she/it 常用 **-th**（**hath**=has, **doth**=does）；对 **thou** 常见 **-st**（**wilt, shouldst** 等）。
    

# 4) 节奏（Iambic Pentameter）

- **iamb（抑扬）**= _da-DUM_；**pentameter**= 5 个抑扬脚 ≈ 10 音节。
    
- 不规则是故意的：句首**trochee（扬抑）**、**extra syllable（多一音节）**、**caesura（中停）**等。
    
- **-ed** 有时读两拍：bless-èd, belov-èd。
    

# 5) 小词速记（Mini-Glossary）

- **ere**=before（在……之前）
    
- **oft**=often（经常）
    
- **anon**=soon（很快）
    
- **whence**=from where（从何处）
    
- **whither**=to where（到何处）
    
- **hence**=from here/therefore（从此/因此，语境判定）
    
- **prove**=test/verify（检验/证明）
    
- **conceit**=extended metaphor（扩展隐喻/奇喻）
    
- **issue**=outcome/offspring（结果/后代）
    
- **physic**=medicine（药/疗方）
    
- **temper**=mix/moderate/character（调和/节制/性情）
    
- **wrack**=ruin（毁坏）
    
- **mark**(v.)=notice（注意/铭记）
    

# 6) “三步翻译法”（最快实用版）

1. **找句子骨架**：Subject–Verb–Object（先不看行尾中断）。
    
2. **去倒装+补省略**：恢复现代语序，补全省掉的小词。
    
3. **逐节意译**：每个 quatrain（四行）→ **1 句中文大意**；couplet 再 **1 句金句/反讽**。  
    （最后再回到英文，写出你自己的简洁英文 paraphrase。）
    

# 7) 迷你示范（Micro-rewrite）

- 原式：**Gives he his truth to Time, that eats all things.**  
    现代序：**He gives his truth to Time, which eats all things.**  
    中文意：把真理交给“时间”（但时间会吞噬一切）。  
    英文释义：People trust Time, yet Time destroys.
    
- 原式：**Ne’er did thy beauty prove my constancy.**  
    现代序：Your beauty never tested my loyalty.  
    中文意：我的忠诚不靠你的美色来“检验”。
    

# 8) 考场/课堂小贴士

- **别逐词硬译**，先抓**论证走向**（第5/9/13行的转折）。
    
- **标出对立**（youth vs. time, truth vs. appearance），容易看出主题张力。
    
- **记双关**：遇到 will/issue/prove 等，先列所有常见义项对照语境。
    
- **句子跨行（enjambment）**很常见，别被行尾迷惑。
